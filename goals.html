What is Wellness?

Health is Wealth. At the Wellness Garden, when we think about wellness, we think about it from the perspective of our mind, body, spirit, emotions, and community. The American College of Lifestyle Medicine looks at six interconnected pillars of health when thinking about our overall health and wellbeing: mental health, substances, sleep, social relationships, nutrition, and movement.

Up until very recently, Mental Health was not considered a very important part of our overall health and wellbeing. Yet, mental health issues contribute to many of the physical health issues that pop up throughout our lifespan. Someone who is super depressed may not have the motivation to live an active lifestyle,socially isolate, and have no hope for their future. They may stop taking care of their physical bodies,disengage from community, and spiral downwards.

You'll find a ton of resources for all of the pillars here. We focus on the mental and social aspects of our health and wellbeing heavily at The Wellness Graden as our team comes from diverse disciplines within the mental and behavioral health field.


What are our goals?

Family

Strengthening Family Relationships

Strengthen family relationships by fostering open, positive communication, setting aside daily distractions for quality time, and establishing meaningful, consistent routines like family meals or game nights. Build bonds by sharing responsibilities, expressing appreciation, showing empathy, and engaging in collaborative activities that foster a sense of belonging.

Facilitating Family Dialogues

Facilitating family dialogues involves creating a safe, structured, and open environment for family members to express thoughts, feelings, and experiences, aiming for better understanding rather than just immediate problem-solving. Effective facilitation focuses on empathy, active listening, and curiosity to build stronger, more resilient relationships.

Youth

Carrer Guidance

Career guidance helps individuals make informed decisions about their education and work by providing self-assessment, career exploration, and planning strategies, involving services like counseling, workshops, and job search assistance to align personal interests, skills, and values with labor market realities for fulfilling career development.

Leaderships Skills

Essential leadership skills include a combination of soft skills, such as emotional intelligence, integrity, and communication, along with strategic abilities like decision-making, delegation, and conflict management. These competencies foster trust, motivate teams, and drive organizational success by enabling leaders to adapt to change and solve complex problems effectively.

Life Skills

Youth life skills are essential, transferable abilities that empower young people to manage daily challenges, make responsible decisions, and foster psychosocial well-being. Key areas include financial literacy (budgeting), practical living (cooking, cleaning), emotional intelligence, communication, and critical thinking.

Addiction Issues

Are you struggling with Drug Addiction?!!

Drug addiction, also called substance use disorder, is a disease that affects a person's brain and behavior and leads to an inability to control the use of a legal or illegal drug or medicine. Substances such as alcohol, marijuana and nicotine also are considered drugs.

Treatments for drug addiction include counseling, medicines, or both. Research shows that combining medicines with counseling gives most people the best chance of success. The counseling may be individual, family, and/or group therapy.

The ultimate goal of The Wellness Garden is therefore to create opportunity for behavior change through helping the drug addicts to curb psychological, physical and emotional problems brought about by the drugs.
